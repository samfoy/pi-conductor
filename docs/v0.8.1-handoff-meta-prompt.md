- **Cost discipline:** critic gates ~$1–3, builder revise loops ~$3–7, designer passes ~$3–5. Plan budget — and prefer `ensemble_send` for revisions over fresh spawns (the prior session's compute was ~$95–100, more than half of which was design + revise loops, not implementation).

## What NOT to do

- **Don't run parallel write-capable personas.** §11 NEW-1 (shipped) prohibits parallel `builder` / `simplifier` spawns because they share `git log` and the working tree even on disjoint files. Harness enforcement is v0.8.1 item 2; until then, the prompt rule is the only guardrail — respect it.
- **Don't keep heavy orchestration narration near a `ensemble_spawn` call** when the spawn uses `inherit_context: filtered` (most personas do). The persona's filtered slice will include your "Spawned X. 3/4 slots used. Holding..." prose and the persona may produce meta-commentary on it instead of executing. Keep the parent's prose terse near spawn points until item 1 lands.
- **Don't write `(see §11)` style forward references** between commit messages or between slices in a multi-slice plan. They ship as dangling text. Either inline the cross-reference content or land the slices in dependency order.
